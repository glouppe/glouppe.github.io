Hi! I am an associate professor in artificial intelligence and deep learning at the University of Liège (Belgium). Previously, I held positions as a research fellow at CERN and as a postdoctoral associate at New York University with the Physics Department and the Center for Data Science.

My research is at the intersection of deep learning, approximate inference and the physical sciences. Together with collaborators, I have been developing a new generation of simulation-based inference algorithms based on deep learning, with several applications in the physical sciences (e.g., in particle physics, astrophysics, astronomy or gravitational wave astronomy). My long-term research objective is to unlock discoveries of a new kind by making AI a cornerstone of the modern scientific method.
